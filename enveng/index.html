Environment and engineering

Given a growing interest in environmental issues, in 2019 I completed a MSc in Environmental Engineering followed by additional courses in mountain development and civil engineering. Since then I had the opportunity of working on academic research project as well as practical project on environmental monitoring.

/ / / / / Research and reports

Peer reviewed articles: I have published my first one about on risk assessment on the reuse of wastewater sludge; currently I am working on a framework on data gathering for Water Security, a risk assessment for Aquaculture workers in Thailand and on a policy briefing for the use of wastewater biosolids as fertilisers.

Coursework on mountain development: A short profile on Ladakh with an overview of key information on natural and socio-economic characteristics; this one looks at recent migration towards Alpine regions.

Coursework in environmental engineering: Detailed design for a WWTP using activated sludge and nutrient removal; this coursework asked to verify the possibility of water abstraction for industrial purpose on a given borehole; results from ground water analysis done in Newcastle.

Coursework in civil engineering: a financial appraisal for possible solutions for the closure of Hammersmith bridge in London; a preliminary structural design for a highrise following EUROCODE
